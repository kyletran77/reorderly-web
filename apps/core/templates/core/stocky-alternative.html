What was Shopify Stocky?

Shopify Stocky was a free inventory management app built and maintained by Shopify. It was available exclusively to Shopify merchants and provided core purchase order management features: creating POs, tracking supplier orders, and managing reorder points per SKU.

Stocky was particularly popular among small to mid-size merchants (20–500 SKUs) who needed basic inventory management without paying for an enterprise solution. At its peak, Stocky had hundreds of thousands of active users across the Shopify ecosystem.

Key features of Stocky included:

  • Manual purchase order creation and tracking
  • Reorder point configuration per SKU
  • Basic supplier contact management
  • Inventory demand forecasting (limited)
  • Integration with Shopify inventory levels
  • Barcode scanning for receiving orders

Stocky’s biggest limitation was that it was entirely manual. Merchants had to log in, check inventory levels, decide when to reorder, and draft their own purchase orders. The app organized the process but didn’t automate it.

Why Shopify discontinued Stocky

Shopify officially delisted Stocky from the App Store in February 2026, with a full shutdown date of August 31, 2026. While Shopify hasn’t provided a detailed public explanation, several factors likely contributed to the decision:

Strategic shift toward AI-native tools

Shopify’s Summer 2025 Editions signaled a clear pivot toward AI-first merchant tools. Shopify Sidekick, their AI commerce assistant, became a core part of the product. Maintaining Stocky — a manual, non-AI tool — didn’t fit this direction. Rather than rebuild Stocky with AI capabilities, Shopify chose to sunset it and let third-party developers fill the gap.

Partner ecosystem strategy

Shopify has historically preferred to let their app partner ecosystem handle niche functionality. With the App Store growing, Shopify likely concluded that inventory management was better served by specialized third-party apps than by a free first-party tool they had to support indefinitely.

Enterprise push

Shopify’s increasing focus on Shopify Plus and enterprise merchants means the company’s development resources are concentrated on features that matter to high-volume businesses — not the basic PO management that Stocky offered.
