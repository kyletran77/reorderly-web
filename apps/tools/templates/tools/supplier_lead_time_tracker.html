Why do suppliers miss their stated lead times?

Suppliers quote lead times based on ideal conditions. In practice, manufacturing delays, raw material shortages, port congestion, and logistics issues regularly push actual delivery times past stated ones. Tracking actual vs. stated helps you build realistic buffers into your reorder points.

What is a good supplier reliability score?

A score of 85+ means the supplier consistently delivers on time. 70–84 means you need to add a few days of buffer stock. Below 70 means the supplier is a stockout risk and you should either find a backup or significantly increase your safety stock.

How does this help with reorder points?

Once you know your supplier's actual average lead time (and variability), use those numbers — not their stated lead time — in your reorder point and safety stock calculations.
