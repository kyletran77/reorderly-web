The gap in Shopify's native PO tools

If you've tried to send a purchase order to a supplier directly from Shopify, you've already found the gap: Shopify doesn't send PO emails.

Shopify's built-in Purchases feature (under Products → Purchase Orders) lets you create a purchase order and receive items against it. But there's no "Send to Supplier" button. No email. No PDF export. You create the PO in Shopify, and then you're on your own to get it to your supplier.

This means most Shopify merchants end up doing one of two things:

  1. Manually copying PO details into an email every time they need to reorder
  2. Managing purchase orders in a spreadsheet or separate app outside Shopify

Both approaches work — until they don't. At higher order volumes, manual PO emails become the bottleneck. SKUs run out because someone forgot to reorder. Suppliers get inconsistent formats. There's no record of what was sent when.

The manual process (and why it breaks down)

Here's how most merchants currently handle supplier POs without automation:

  1. Notice a product is running low (by checking inventory manually or getting a Shopify low-stock alert)
  2. Decide how much to order
  3. Write an email to the supplier with product names, SKUs, quantities, and requested delivery date
  4. Wait for supplier confirmation
  5. When goods arrive, manually update inventory in Shopify

This works fine when you have 5–10 SKUs and one or two suppliers. It breaks down when:

  • You have 30+ SKUs that need regular replenishment
  • Different products come from different suppliers with different lead times
  • You're traveling, sick, or distracted and miss a reorder window
  • You need a record of what was ordered, when, and for how much
Note: Shopify Stocky handled this workflow for hundreds of thousands of merchants — but Stocky is being shut down on August 31, 2026. Merchants who relied on it need a new solution.

What a supplier PO email needs to include

Before automating anything, it helps to know what a complete purchase order email should contain. Your supplier needs enough information to fulfill the order without follow-up questions:

  • PO number — a unique reference ID for tracking
  • Order date — when the PO was issued
  • Expected delivery date — based on the supplier's lead time
  • Line items — product name, SKU, quantity ordered, unit cost
  • Total order value
  • Shipping address — where to send the goods
  • Payment terms — Net 30, due on receipt, etc.
  • Your contact information

Most manual PO emails miss at least two or three of these. Suppliers deal with it, but it creates unnecessary back-and-forth and slows down fulfillment.

How to automate PO emails to suppliers

The cleanest way to automate supplier PO emails for a Shopify store is to use an inventory management app that:

  1. Stores your supplier information (email, lead time, MOQ, payment terms)
  2. Monitors your inventory levels against per-SKU reorder points
  3. Creates a purchase order when a SKU hits its reorder threshold
  4. Emails the PO to the supplier automatically
