What's actually happening with Shopify Stocky

Shopify Stocky — the free inventory management and purchase order app used by hundreds of thousands of Shopify merchants — is being permanently shut down. Shopify removed it from the App Store on February 2, 2026, meaning no new merchants can install it. Existing users received notices that the app will stop working entirely on August 31, 2026.

Stocky was originally a product called "Handshake," acquired by Shopify in 2019. For seven years it handled a critical gap in Shopify's native toolset: purchase order creation, supplier management, reorder point tracking, and basic demand forecasting. Shopify never fully integrated these features into its core admin, and now it's discontinuing the third-party solution without a native equivalent.

Shopify has pointed merchants toward its Purchases feature (basic PO creation, no supplier email automation) and a list of third-party apps. Neither fills the full gap that Stocky covered.

⚠️ Important: "August 31" is not a soft deadline. The app will stop functioning — not just stop receiving updates. Any in-progress purchase orders, supplier workflows, or inventory counts that depend on Stocky will break on that date.

Key dates you need to know

  • February 2, 2026: Stocky removed from the Shopify App Store. No new installs allowed.
  • Now through August 2026: Existing installs continue to work (with reduced support).
  • August 31, 2026: Stocky shuts down completely. All functionality stops.

That gives you roughly 5 months from today (March 2026) to export your data and migrate to a replacement. That sounds like a lot of time — but merchants who wait until July will be competing for onboarding slots at the same apps at the same time, during peak summer operations for most retail businesses.

What you'll lose on August 31 if you haven't migrated

This isn't just losing access to a UI. Here's what disappears on August 31:

Your purchase order history

Every PO you've ever created through Stocky — sent dates, quantities, supplier confirmations, receiving records — lives in Stocky's database. When the app shuts down, that history is gone. For some merchants this is years of procurement records that may have accounting or audit implications.

Your supplier database

Every supplier you've set up in Stocky — with their contact email, MOQs, lead times, payment terms, and notes — needs to be manually exported and re-entered into your new system. There's no automatic transfer.

Your reorder thresholds

If you've spent time calibrating reorder points and order quantities for each SKU, that configuration lives in Stocky and has to be exported before shutdown.

Any open purchase orders

POs you've created but not yet received — if they're still open when Stocky shuts down, you lose visibility into them from within the app. Your supplier may still ship the goods, but you'll have no tracking record.

What to do right now (takes about 30 minutes)

Step 1: Export your purchase order history

  1. Open Stocky from your Shopify admin
  2. Go to Reports → Purchase Orders
  3. Set date range to All time
  4. Click Export CSV — save as stocky-po-history.csv

Step 2: Document your suppliers

  1. Go to Suppliers in Stocky
  2. For each supplier, copy: name, email, contact name, phone, payment terms, MOQ, lead time
  3. Paste into a Google Sheet — this becomes your migration import file

Step 3: Export your reorder rules

  1. Go to Reports → Replenishment in Stocky
  2. Export the replenishment report — it contains your per-SKU reorder points and quantities
  3. If export isn't available, screenshot or manually document the thresholds for your top 20 SKUs at minimum

Step 4: Start evaluating replacements now

Give yourself 4–6 weeks to evaluate a replacement before you have to fully commit. That means starting today, not in June.

Why you shouldn't wait until summer

Three real risks if you wait until July or August to migrate:

1. Supplier disruption during your busiest period. Most retail businesses have high-volume seasons (back-to-school, holiday, summer). Switching your procurement workflow mid-season is high-risk. Migrate now, when operations are quieter.

2. Everyone else will be migrating at the same time. Thousands of Stocky merchants will all start evaluating replacements in June and July. Onboarding support will be stretched at every competing app. You'll get better setup help if you start now.

3. Your data window is closing. The longer you wait to export from Stocky, the more historical context you lose. Demand forecasting in your new tool will be less accurate if it has to start from scratch versus importing 2+ years of purchase history.

The merchants who handle this well are the ones who treat it as routine infrastructure maintenance done early — not a crisis solved at the deadline. You have the time. Use it.
